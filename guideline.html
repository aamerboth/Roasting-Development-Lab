Heat/Power Setting

Roaster Preheat

The first roast of the day typically behaves much differently from all subsequent roasts. Take this into consideration when planning your roasting for the day.

One approach is to let the roaster warm up for significantly longer than usual. In my experience, the bullet warms up in 10 minutes but I have heard of roasters who will let their roasters warm up for 20 or 30 minutes.

Personally, I am usually trying to get in and roast as much coffee as I can without letting my roaster sit and warm up. To adjust for this I usually roast my least sensitive coffee right away. If I have a dense coffee or if I am planning on doing a medium roast (the darkest I will roast) then I do that first. These roasts are the least sensitive to heat and are the most forgiving.
